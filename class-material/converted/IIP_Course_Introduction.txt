Course Description

                   Why do countries go to war? How do alliances function? How do histories
                   of conflict shape conflict in the current system?
                   Why do states open their borders to trade? Does the economic
                   interdependence strengthen cultural ties between countries? Can
                   multi-national corporations (MNCs) shape international politics?
                   How do social movements influence international politics? What are
                   international organizations, and what is the role of international
                   organizations such as the World Trade Organization (WTO) or the United
                   States (UN) in international politics?
                   Does UN peacekeeping work? What is International Humanitarian Law
                   (IHL), and how relevant is IHL to international politics today?
                   What causes civil war? How do countries address the threat of terrorism?
                   What shapes countries’ responses to issues such as the COVID-19
                   pandemic? What conditions would foster greater cooperation on climate
                   change?
