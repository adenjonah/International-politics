Race and IR




                 Race is understood as a socialized identifier based on a perception of
                 relative superiority/inferiority of groups falling under that identifier
                 The meaning of physical differences are socialized and can manifest
                 differently in various societies
                 Example: The concept of ”whiteness” and ”blackness” may differ for the
                 same group in the Brazilian versus the U.S. context (and by era)

         Race and IR (cont’d) -
                 Race and the racialization of international politics has occurred both as
                 a factor in imperialism and as a product of it (in today’s focus - IPE, e.g.,
                 World Systems Theory).
                 Examples: (as factor) League of Nations’ role in encouraging competitive
                 imperialism in Africa, (as product) Rwandan Genocide/and response
