         The BP Model -
                 a state’s foreign policy is the outcome of internal politics
                 actors within the government can exert greater or lesser influence on a
                 state’s foreign policy depending on its relative power within the
                 government
                 and actors may also be motivated by organization-based or personal
                 interests. (”Where you stand is where you sit.”)
