         Buck passing -
                 States ”pass the buck” when they fail to respond to a rising great power
                 in hopes that another state more threatened by the rising power will act
                 typically by great power who have geographic barriers/distance from a
                 rising power

         Geography -
                 The impact of a state’s military power diminishes over distance and other

                 decreasing its likelihood when technology favors defense

         The absence of a central authority (i.e., anarchy) means that states constantly
         seek to ”help themselves” against the potential they are threatened by other
         states.

                 In addition to power considerations and structural modifiers, states are left
                 to assume the intentions of another.
                 Conflict may arise between states as a result of misinterpretations of states
                 actions, rather than true intentions to aggress.
                 To avoid misinterpretations (later lecture), states may signal their
                 intentions.
