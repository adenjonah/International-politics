# CIA's Former Counterterrorism Chief on Afghanistan: A Policy Failure, Not Intelligence Failure

**By Douglas London** — August 18, 2021

## Overview

Douglas London, former CIA Counterterrorism Chief for South and Southwest Asia, argues that the rapid collapse of Afghanistan resulted from political decision-making rather than intelligence failures. He contends that intelligence agencies accurately warned both Trump and Biden administrations about potential outcomes.

## Key Arguments

**Intelligence Provided Accurate Warnings**

London states that the CIA assessed multiple scenarios for Afghanistan's future. The intelligence community projected various timelines -- "30 days from withdrawal to collapse? 60? 18 months?" -- depending on policy choices. Critically, assessments highlighted that Afghan forces could capitulate "within days" under the circumstances that ultimately occurred.

**Political Over Strategic Decisions**

Both administrations were "held captive" by the "ending Forever Wars" campaign slogan, allowing political considerations to override intelligence assessments. The decision to withdraw was predetermined regardless of warnings about alarming outcomes.

**The Doha Agreement Problems**

Secretary of State Mike Pompeo championed the February 2020 Taliban withdrawal agreement despite intelligence warnings that its two main objectives -- securing Taliban commitments to break with al-Qaeda and pursue peaceful conflict resolution -- were "highly unlikely."

## Taliban's Strategic Advantages

London explains the Taliban's effective approach:
- Used a "shadow government" structure maintaining communication networks across provinces
- Negotiated and purchased territory rather than fighting directly
- Deployed sophisticated propaganda machines that manipulated media coverage
- Received financial support from Pakistan, Russia, Iran, and Gulf countries
- Coordinated with al-Qaeda members released from Bagram detention

## Afghan Government Weaknesses

President Ashraf Ghani's policies undermined government resilience:
- His refusal to engage in traditional Afghan political patronage weakened support
- Dismantling warlords' private armies removed incentives for government loyalty
- Afghan military and political leaders hedged their positions as U.S. withdrawal became apparent

## Conclusion

The Afghanistan collapse represented a failure of leadership judgment rather than intelligence analysis. "The failure was not due to any lack of warning, but rather the hubris and political risk calculus of decision makers" prioritizing personal and political interests over national security considerations.
