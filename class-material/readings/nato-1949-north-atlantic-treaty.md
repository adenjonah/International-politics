# The North Atlantic Treaty

Washington D.C. -- April 4, 1949

### Preamble

The parties reaffirm their commitment to the UN Charter and desire for peace. They are determined to protect freedom, democratic principles, individual liberty, and the rule of law. They seek stability in the North Atlantic region and agree to unite for collective defense.

### Article 1
Parties commit to resolving disputes peacefully and refraining from threats or force inconsistent with UN purposes.

### Article 2
Parties will strengthen free institutions, promote understanding of democratic principles, and encourage economic collaboration to maintain stability.

### Article 3
Each party will maintain and develop capacity to resist armed attack through continuous self-help and mutual assistance.

### Article 4
Parties will consult when any member's territorial integrity, political independence, or security is threatened.

### Article 5
An armed attack on one member in Europe or North America constitutes an attack on all. Members agree to provide assistance through individual or collective self-defense, including armed force if necessary. Actions taken shall be reported to the UN Security Council and terminated once the Council addresses the situation.

### Article 6
Article 5 protections extend to member territories in Europe or North America, occupation forces stationed at treaty entry, the Mediterranean Sea, and the North Atlantic area north of the Tropic of Cancer.

### Article 7
The treaty does not affect UN Charter rights or the Security Council's primary responsibility for maintaining peace.

### Article 8
Members declare their existing international commitments don't conflict with this treaty and pledge not to enter conflicting engagements.

### Article 9
Members establish a Council to oversee treaty implementation, capable of meeting promptly. A defense committee shall recommend implementation measures.

### Article 10
Members may unanimously invite any European state able to further treaty principles and contribute to North Atlantic security to accede to the treaty.

### Article 11
Parties shall ratify according to their constitutional processes.

### Article 12
After ten years, parties may consult to review the treaty based on current peace and security factors.

### Article 13
After twenty years, any party may withdraw one year after notifying the U.S. government.

### Article 14
English and French texts are equally authentic.
