# Finland Joins NATO as 31st Ally

Finland officially became NATO's newest member on April 4, 2023, completing its accession process by depositing the instrument of accession with the United States at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. This milestone followed the NATO Allies' signing of Finland's Accession Protocol on July 5, 2022, and subsequent ratification by all 30 member parliaments.

## Accession Ceremony

NATO Secretary General Jens Stoltenberg welcomed Finland during a formal flag-raising ceremony attended by Finnish President Sauli Niinisto and Foreign Minister Pekka Haavisto. The U.S. Secretary of State Antony Blinken accepted the accession documents on behalf of the United States government.

Stoltenberg expressed his satisfaction, stating that the addition strengthens the Alliance while demonstrating that Russian President Putin's objectives failed. He remarked: "Instead of less NATO, he has achieved the opposite; more NATO and our door remains firmly open."

## Leadership and Strategic Impact

The Secretary General commended President Niinisto's leadership in bringing Finland into the Alliance, describing NATO as "the most successful Alliance in history." He emphasized that Finland's accession benefits the country, Nordic security, and NATO collectively.

## Military Strengths

Finland brings substantial military capabilities and exceptional resilience to NATO. Finnish and NATO troops had previously collaborated as partners for years before achieving full allied status.
