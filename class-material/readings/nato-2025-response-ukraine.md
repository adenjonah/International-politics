# NATO's Support for Ukraine

## Overview

NATO condemns Russia's "brutal and unprovoked war of aggression against Ukraine in the strongest possible terms." The alliance has coordinated unprecedented military and non-lethal assistance to help Ukraine defend itself, with NATO Allies providing billions of euros in equipment, supplies, training, and critical support since 2022.

## Key Support Activities

### Prioritised Ukraine Requirements List (PURL)

NATO coordinates purchases of critical defense equipment from the United States for other NATO members. Under this initiative, NATO's Supreme Allied Commander identifies needed packages, and Allies commit funding. More than $4 billion in military equipment has been committed through PURL, with deliveries underway.

### NATO Security Assistance and Training for Ukraine (NSATU)

Based in Wiesbaden, Germany, NSATU coordinates weapons delivery and training of Ukrainian forces. The organization employs nearly 700 personnel from Allied and partner nations and manages anti-tank systems, air defense equipment, artillery, drones, tanks, and fighter jets.

### Comprehensive Assistance Package (CAP)

Allies have pledged over EUR 1.2 billion for non-lethal aid including cold-weather clothing, body armor, fuel, secure communications, medical supplies, and demining equipment. Multi-year assistance programs support Ukraine's transition to NATO standards.

### Long-Term Security Assistance Pledge

NATO committed to minimum baseline funding of EUR 40 billion in 2024. Allies exceeded this substantially, providing over EUR 50 billion that year, with nearly 60% from European Allies and Canada. Additional EUR 35 billion in security assistance has been committed for 2025.

### NATO-Ukraine Council (NUC)

This joint body enables political dialogue and equal participation between Allies and Ukraine, advancing Ukraine's NATO membership aspirations and serving as a crisis consultation mechanism.

## Historical Context

NATO's support predates 2022. Following Russia's 2014 annexation of Crimea and Donbas occupation, NATO helped reform Ukraine's armed forces with equipment, financial support, and training for tens of thousands of troops.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Ukraine a NATO member?** Ukraine is not a NATO member but serves as a NATO partner country with close cooperation outside the Article 5 security guarantee.

**Will Ukraine join NATO?** NATO member states agreed at the 2008 Bucharest Summit on Ukraine's eventual membership. The 2023 Vilnius Summit removed the Membership Action Plan requirement, streamlining the process.

**Why isn't NATO sending troops or closing skies?** NATO's defensive posture aims to prevent escalation. Enforcing no-fly zones or deploying combat troops would bring NATO into direct conflict with Russia, significantly escalating the war.

**NATO's Position on Peace Negotiations:** NATO welcomes efforts toward "a just and lasting peace as soon as possible." Ukraine must negotiate from strength, requiring continued NATO military assistance.

**NATO's Position on Occupied Territories:** NATO strongly supports Ukraine's sovereignty and territorial integrity within internationally recognized borders. The alliance condemns Russia's 2014 Crimea annexation and 2022 attempted annexation of Donetsk, Luhansk, Kherson, and Zaporizhzhia regions.

**NATO's Defense Strengthening:** Following Russia's 2022 full-scale invasion, NATO activated defense plans and deployed thousands of additional troops. The alliance maintains 500,000 troops at high readiness across all domains.

**The 5% GDP Defense Commitment:** Allies have pledged to invest 5% of GDP on defense by 2035.

**Response to Nuclear and Chemical Weapons Threats:** NATO takes Russia's "threatening nuclear rhetoric" seriously. The alliance warns that "nuclear war cannot be won and should never be fought."
